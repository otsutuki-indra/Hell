04 DATA SHARING & DISCLOSURE

We do not sell personal information. Sharing occurs only:

  • Service Providers: AWS/GCP (hosting), Stripe (payments), SendGrid (email), GA/Hotjar (analytics), Linear/Notion (PM), GitHub (CI/CD) — all bound by contractual data protection obligations.

  • Business Transfers: M&A, asset sales, bankruptcy. Affected individuals notified via email or prominent notice.
  • Protection of Rights: Hellcorp's or public safety/security incidents/legal proceedings.
  • With Consent: Any other disclosure requires explicit consent.

06 DATA SECURITY

Aligned with NIST CSF 2.0 and SOC 2 Type II controls:

  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit.
  • Access: RBAC, least privilege, MFA required for all systems.
  • Infrastructure: AWS/GCP, VPC isolation, WAF, DDoS (Cloudflare/AWS Shield).
  • Monitoring: 24/7 SIEM, automated anomaly detection, IR playbooks.
  • Personnel: Background checks, security training, NDAs, quarterly access reviews.
  • Testing: Annual third-party pentesting, continuous vulnerability scanning, bug bounty.
  • Incident Response: Documented IRP, defined roles/escalation, 72-hour notification capability.

No system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a security incident, we will notify affected individuals and regulators per applicable breach notification laws.

03 DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS (GDPR)

  • Verification: Identity verified via two-factor method before fulfilling requests.
  • Timeline: One month from receipt. Extended by two months for complex requests — notified within first month with reasons.
  • Format: Responses provided in writing (electronic unless otherwise requested). Portable data in JSON/CSV.
  • Refusal: Right to refuse manifestly unfounded or excessive requests, with justification. Right to charge reasonable fee for repetitive requests.
  • Erasure exceptions: Legal obligation, establishment/defense of legal claims, public interest, archiving in public interest, research/statistics with appropriate safeguards.
  • Automated decisions: When solely automated decisions with legal effects occur, right to request human intervention, express point of view, and contest the decision.

04 INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS (EU)

  • SCCs: Commission Decision 2021/914 — module appropriate to transfer type (C-C, C-P, P-P, P-C). Supplementary measures per Schrems II.
  • TIA Process: (1) Map transfer — what data, to whom, for what purpose; (2) Assess destination country legal framework; (3) Identify gaps; (4) Implement supplementary measures; (5) Document and review periodically.
  • Supplementary measures: End-to-end encryption, pseudonymization before transfer, split processing (no single country receives complete identifiable dataset), contractual commitments from importer.
  • Adequacy: Active monitoring of Commission adequacy decisions. Immediate reassessment if adequacy decision is challenged or revoked.

07 DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENTS

DPIAs required and conducted for:

  • Large-scale processing of special category data (Art. 9).
  • Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas on a large scale.
  • AI/ML systems making or supporting decisions with legal/significant effects on individuals.
  • Processing of biometric data for identification purposes.
  • Cross-border transfers to countries without adequacy decisions where supplementary measures are novel or complex.

DPIAs follow EDPB guidelines: describe processing, assess necessity/proportionality, identify and mitigate risks, consult DPO, review if processing changes.

08 DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENTS

  • Sub-processor engagement conditions (prior specific/general authorization, same contractual obligations flowing down).

Global Privacy. This section covers jurisdictions beyond US and EU: UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Brazil, India, UAE, and others. US policy serves as baseline, supplemented by jurisdiction-specific requirements below.

01 JURISDICTION-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS

United Kingdom

  • UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018. Hellcorp UK Ltd. is UK representative under Article 27.
  • UK DPC equivalents: Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, SK9 5AF — ico.org.uk
  • Data subject rights identical to EU GDPR with UK-specific modifications.
  • International transfers: UK adequacy (EEA), IDTA, or UK addendum to EU SCCs for third countries.
  • PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations) govern electronic marketing and cookies.

Canada

  • PIPEDA: 10 fair information principles — accountability, identifying purposes, consent, limiting collection, limiting use/disclosure/retention, accuracy, safeguards, openness, individual access, challenging compliance.
  • Quebec Law 25: Privacy impact assessments mandatory. Breach notification to Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec (CAIQ) and affected individuals "as soon as feasible." DPO designation required. Automatic profiling requires consent.
  • PIPA (AB/BC): Similar principles for provincial private-sector organizations.
  • Complaints: Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada — priv.gc.ca

Australia

  • Privacy Act 1988 + Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). APP 1–13 govern collection, use, disclosure, data quality, cross-border disclosure, security, access, correction, identifiers, anonymity, transborder flows, APP entity accountability.
  • NDB scheme: Eligible data breaches notified to OAIC and affected individuals when "likely to result in serious harm." Assessment within 30 days.
  • Privacy Act Reform (2024+): Monitoring proposed reforms including Fair and Reasonable test, expanded APP coverage, increased penalties (up to AUD 50M+).
  • Complaints: OAIC — oaic.gov.au

Japan

  • APPI (amended 2022): Purpose limitation, data minimization, security measures. Individual numbers (My Number) subject to strict handling rules.
  • Cross-border transfers: data subject consent, or equivalent measures recognized by PPC (including countries with adequacy, APEC CBPR, or contractual arrangements meeting PPC standards).
  • PPC complaint handling: ppc.go.jp

Singapore

  • PDPA 2012 (amended 2020, 2024): Consent as default, with legitimate interests exception for certain business purposes (2024 amendment). Mandatory breach notification within 3 calendar days of assessment.
  • Cross-border: comparable protection standard or data subject consent. PDPC publishes list of countries/organizations meeting standard.
  • Data portability obligation for organizations of prescribed size.
  • Complaints: PDPC — pdpc.gov.sg

South Korea

  • PIPA: Consent required for collection, use, provision of personal information. Explicit consent for sensitive information. Cross-border transfer consent + notification of recipient country's protection level.
  • Breach notification to PIPC within 72 hours (1,000+ affected individuals) or 72 hours (sensitive data of any number).
  • Automated profiling: right to request suspension and human review.
  • Complaints: PIPC — pipc.go.kr

Brazil

  • LGPD: 10 legal bases (Art. 7) — consent, legal obligation, public policy execution, research, contract execution, exercise of rights, protection of life, health protection, legitimate interest, credit protection.
  • DPO (Encarregado) mandatory. ANPD registration for certain controllers.
  • International transfers (Art. 33): adequacy decision, SCCs, BCRs, seals/certificates, contractual clauses, consent, international cooperation, ICO.
  • Administrative sanctions: warnings, fines (up to 2% of revenue, cap BRL 50M per infraction), publicization, blocking/deletion.
  • Complaints: ANPD — gov.br/anpd

India

  • DPDPA 2023: Consent-based framework. "Deemed consent" for certain legitimate uses. Data principal rights: access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, nomination.
  • Significant data fiduciaries: appoint DPO, conduct DPIAs, periodic audits.
  • Cross-border: government-maintained whitelist of permissible countries (notified separately). Non-whitelisted transfers restricted except with explicit consent + additional safeguards.
  • Data Protection Board of India for enforcement.

United Arab Emirates

  • Federal Decree-Law No. 45/2021: Consent-based, purpose limitation, data minimization, security obligations, breach notification, DPO for certain controllers, cross-border transfer conditions.
  • DIFC Law No. 5/2020: Aligns closely with GDPR. Commissioner of Data Protection — difc.gov.ae
  • ADGM DPRE 2021: GDPR-equivalent regime. Office of Data Protection — adgm.com

03 CROSS-BORDER DATA FLOWS (GLOBAL)

For all jurisdictions, Hellcorp implements a cross-border transfer framework:

  • Transfer Impact Assessment before initiating any new cross-border data flow.
  • Appropriate transfer mechanism selected (SCCs, adequacy, consent, BCRs, IDTA, contractual arrangements per local law).
  • Supplementary technical measures (encryption, pseudonymization, key management in home jurisdiction) where destination country assessment reveals gaps.
  • Periodic reassessment (at least annually or upon legal/regulatory changes).
